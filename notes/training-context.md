## Goal vs Reality Assessment

### Sub-3:00 (4:15/km race pace)
- Requires VO2max ~63 (currently 50 — gap of 13 points)
- Requires half marathon ~1:22–1:24 (currently 1:38 — gap of 14–16 min)
- Requires tempo pace 4:00–4:05/km (currently ~4:25–4:35/km)
- Requires peak weekly volume 90–120km (current plan peaks at 75km)
- **Honest assessment:** Very unlikely in 10 months given inconsistent base and VO2max gap. VO2max gains of 13 points in 10 months would require extraordinary improvement.

### Sub-3:15 (4:37/km race pace)
- Requires VO2max ~57–58
- Requires consistent 80–90km/week
- **Honest assessment:** Stretch but realistic with excellent training consistency. Best realistic outcome.

### Sub-3:30 (4:58/km race pace)
- Current fitness already predicts ~3:21–3:25 from race data
- Very achievable with consistent training
- **This is the floor — should happen on a good day regardless**

## Recommended Goal Hierarchy
1. **A goal (dream):** Sub-3:00
2. **B goal (realistic stretch):** Sub-3:15
3. **C goal (floor):** Sub-3:30

Train for A, race for B, accept C as worst case.

## Triple Goal Conflict
Running sub-3:00 + gaining muscle + losing fat are partially contradictory. Priority order:
1. **Running performance** (primary training stressor)
2. **Fat loss** (let running create deficit — don't actively cut hard)
3. **Muscle/strength** (maintain, not maximize — gym is supportive, not primary)

Key: Don't cut calories aggressively. High protein (~160–165g/day) to preserve muscle. Let 90km+/week training naturally drop BF. Cutting hard while running high volume = poor recovery, injury, and no performance gains.
